F10

The Ferrari F10, introduced for the 2010 Formula 1 season, exemplifies Ferrari’s dedication to precision engineering and competitive excellence. As a direct successor to the F60, the F10 was designed to compete at the highest level of motorsport, incorporating cutting-edge technology and innovative design elements to challenge for the championship.

Driven by Fernando Alonso and Felipe Massa, the Ferrari F10 demonstrated strong competitiveness throughout the 2010 season. Alonso, in particular, showcased his driving prowess, securing five Grand Prix victories and consistently finishing on the podium. His remarkable performances kept Ferrari in the hunt for the Drivers' Championship until the final race of the season, underscoring the F10’s capabilities.

Today, the Ferrari F10 is remembered as a symbol of innovation and determination. It represents a crucial period in Ferrari’s racing history, embodying the relentless pursuit of performance and excellence that defines the Scuderia. The F10 remains a celebrated and admired machine among fans and collectors, reflecting Ferrari’s enduring legacy in Formula 1.
