126 CK

The Ferrari 126 CK, unveiled in 1981, marks a significant milestone in Ferrari's racing history as the team's first Formula 1 car to feature a turbocharged engine. Designed to compete in the turbo era of Grand Prix racing, the 126 CK represented a bold leap forward in both technology and performance.

The design of the Ferrari 126 CK was a fusion of aerodynamic efficiency and Italian elegance. Its streamlined bodywork, featuring distinctive side pods for enhanced cooling and aerodynamics, was crafted to maximize downforce and minimize drag. The iconic Ferrari red livery, adorned with sponsor decals and the prancing horse emblem, showcased the car's aggressive yet sophisticated aesthetic.

Today, the Ferrari 126 CK stands as a testament to Ferrari's pioneering spirit and commitment to pushing the boundaries of racing excellence. It remains a cherished icon among collectors and enthusiasts, celebrated for its revolutionary design and enduring legacy in Formula 1 history.
