F1-2000

The Ferrari F1-2000, introduced for the 2000 Formula 1 season, marks a historic moment for Scuderia Ferrari as it ushered in a new era of dominance. This car played a pivotal role in ending Ferrari’s 21-year drought by clinching both the Drivers' and Constructors' Championships, setting the stage for one of the most successful periods in the team’s storied history.

The Ferrari F1-2000 was driven by the legendary Michael Schumacher and his teammate Rubens Barrichello. Schumacher’s exceptional skill and relentless determination, combined with Barrichello’s solid support, proved to be a winning combination. Schumacher secured nine victories throughout the season, including a dramatic win at the Japanese Grand Prix, which sealed the Drivers' Championship for him and the Constructors' Championship for Ferrari.

Today, the Ferrari F1-2000 is celebrated as a legendary machine that symbolizes the resurgence of Ferrari’s prowess in Formula 1. It remains an icon of engineering excellence, competitive spirit, and the relentless pursuit of perfection that defines the Ferrari legacy.
